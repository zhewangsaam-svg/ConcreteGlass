Case Study Report

Concrete and Glass: Performance and Maintenance Case Study

The case study of the report is the Darma Tower, one of the residential development landmarks in the regeneration area of London in Nine Elms. It consists of two tower-connected buildings one of 50 stories, about 170 meters high, and a tower on 23 stories, with 360 luxury apartments. The podium level has commercial and leisure multifunctional areas. The construction of the building was done in 2022 and is an important part of the modern skyscape of London.

The building is primarily residential in nature, which uses a reinforced concrete frame and glazing envelope system, which is curtain wall (British Standards Institution, 2013; British Standards Institution, 2016a). The structure has a modern minimalistic design that is made up of concrete and glasses envelope with an architecture matching the practical requirement of high-density housing.

2. Material Selection Criteria

2.1 Selection of Concrete

Considering the Damac Tower high-rise residential building in London as an example, this multi-story residential building needs a design that could withstand vertical construction loads in the long run (i.e., floor slab dead weight, furniture, and occupant loads) and mainly manage the horizontal loads due to the wind actions.

With these high residences, reinforced concrete mechanization provides high load-bearing quality and general stiffness. This is a very effective measure to control inter-story lateral displacement and vibration comfort, as well as achieve the stresses and deformations requirements of primary elements such as columns, walls, cores and floor slabs.

In terms of fire resistance, the non-combustibility of concrete, structural constraints such as the size of the member and the protection layer can easily help reach the intended fire resistance rating needed by the residential building.

Concrete, throughout its development, has an established supply chain and rather manageable prices in terms of the duration and its maintenance. It has good durability in the rainy and humid environment of London, which is a highly waterproof and stature-wise secure and stable, through the appropriate protective coatings, waterproofing and elaborate detail, thus it occurs to save a lot of money on long-term maintenance.

As a result, concrete was chosen as core structural material, to balance the structural safety, efficiency of the construction and long-term values of the asset.

2.2 Glass Selection

Large-scale application of glass is oriented to functionality and beauty: the glass curtain walls get maximum natural daylights in order to create bright houses; the double-, triple-pane glass units are required to guarantee light enough and correspond to the thermal and acoustic standards.

The glass envelope system has been found to be very effective in enhancing light in the interiors and lowering the cost of artificial lighting. The double-paned insulated glass is not only thermostatically insulated but also soundproofed and thus fits the demand of the comfort of apartments nowadays.

The curtain walls of the buildings are glass-made which aesthetically gives the buildings an air of clean, contemporary look which goes well with the other architectural designs around them. Interior common spaces are partitioned with glass levels to increase transparency and openness.

As a precaution, the tempered and laminated safety glass is followed by the high-density residential standards to avoid injuries caused by broken glass.

Climate in London is usually modest and there is relatively high rainfall where horizons forces mostly prevail on high rise structures. The critical control points of the concrete components under such environmental and usage conditions usually revolve around service performance/deflection/crack control, construction quality/vibration and curing and can be subjected to joint sealing continuity in humid conditions.

There is relatively even temperature and humid environment in indoor areas but columns, beams and floor slabs still must support self-weight and live loads as well as comply with deformation and cracking restrictions during normal service life. Poor compaction in the construction or incomplete curing may cause the surface defect such as honeycombing and bugholes, which may result in a lack of durability.

The lack of humidity in basements and such areas is less likely. Detailed consideration of the integrity of closures and surface protection should be given to such details like slab undersides and joints/sealing lines.

Exterior envelope components are directly exposed to rain and wind. The continuity of construction joints and node seals significantly impacts water infiltration risks and the thermal performance of the envelope structure. Therefore, material durability and node detailing practices should be controlled and verified according to applicable code requirements.

4. Maintenance or performance issues

4.1 Concrete Repair

(a)

In field photos, there are continuous linear lines of sealing under the slab of the basement, and vertical lines of sealing are also observed in the corner of the walls and columns. On the wall, we can see the evidence of treatment of the surface, dressing hope holes and local patching.

Since the environment in London is humid and the use condition is fairly long-run, any discontinuity in these sealing lines or localized failure in the coating can permit the migration of moisture along interfaces within localized damp patches. This enhances faster coating chalking, edge lifting as well as high demands of secondary repair. The main effects of such problems are in the form of reduced durability and increased maintenance rate, without having a direct reflection of structure safety concern in the current observation.

Conclusion

This report compares two basic construction materials concrete and glass which are used in the London apartments by arriving at the following important conclusions:

The standards to be adhered during the selection of materials used in apartments need to be very strict in terms of structural safety, functional comfort and cost control. Concrete frame buildings can satisfy the load bearing and fireproofing needs of multi-storey buildings whereas the glass envelope building system meets functional goals of day- lighting, thermal insulation, and soundproofing. A combination of such materials is one of the best options that can be suggested in situations of high density residential cases in apartments.

The quality of construction and environmental ageing is usually associated with the maintenance problems with building materials. Such issues as the cracks in the concreteness should be addressed by the standard construction methods and frequent maintenance in order to prolong the life of the building.

In this case study, it is shown that the key characteristic of construction engineering is to align material selection with the performance requirements. Structural safety, residential comfort and durability can only be combined by incorporating building functionality, regional climate and use situations. That is because long-term results of useful materials such as ceramics, glass, or other materials impose an irreversible constraint on material performance, despite the careful design and construction process, as can be observed in the field.
